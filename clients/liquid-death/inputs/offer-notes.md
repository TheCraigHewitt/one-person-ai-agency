# Offer Notes

## What The Product Does

It is water — still, sparkling, or iced tea — in a tall-boy aluminum can. Functionally identical to any other premium water. The product is the brand wrapper.

## Who It Is For

People who think bottled water marketing is embarrassing and want a drink that does not announce them as a wellness participant. Heavy lean on 21-40 men who go to the gym, festivals, or bars and don't want a Bud Light or a Smartwater.

## Why People Buy

- Identity signal — the can does work in the hand at the gym, the show, the cookout
- Anti-plastic — aluminum is infinitely recyclable, brand donates % to plastic-kill charity
- Tastes fine — table stakes, not the reason
- Subscription removes the friction of remembering to reorder

## Proof Available

- Trade press: brand hit $263M revenue in 2023 (public reporting)
- Heavy retail presence — Whole Foods, Target, 7-Eleven, Sheetz
- Celebrity/creator collabs: Bert Kreischer, Steve-O, Tony Hawk, Martha Stewart
- B Corp certification, 10% to charity (Liquid Death Foundation)

## Objections

- "It is just water" → yes, that is the joke
- "It is expensive for water" → unit economics on a multi-pack are closer to LaCroix than people assume
- "I don't drink alcohol so I don't need the alternative" → reframe to gym / hike / "I am sick of plastic bottles"
- "The branding is gross to me" → not the target customer, do not chase

## CTA

"Get your first 8-pack delivered." Subscribe-and-save is the long-term lever, but the first ask is just the trial pack.
