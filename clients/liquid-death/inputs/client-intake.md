# Client Intake

Client: Liquid Death
Website: liquiddeath.com
Industry: DTC beverage / mountain water + iced tea + sparkling
Primary offer: Tall-boy canned still water, sparkling water, and "Armless Palmer" iced tea sold in 8-packs and 12-packs DTC plus retail (Whole Foods, 7-Eleven, Target, Sheetz)
Price / pricing model: ~$15.99 per 8-pack DTC, subscribe-and-save ~$13.59. AOV target ~$40 with multi-pack bundles.
Target buyer: 21-40, anti-Big-Beverage aesthetic, gym / festival / sober-curious overlap, irreverent humor sensibility
Sales motion: Paid social → DTC site (Shopify) → first-order discount → subscription. Retail handled separately.
Current marketing channels: Meta (Facebook + Instagram), TikTok organic + spark ads, YouTube pre-roll, partnerships (Bert Kreischer, Steve-O, Tony Hawk, Martha Stewart skits), retail co-marketing
Meta ads objective: New customer acquisition for DTC subscription. Secondary: pulling lapsed subscribers back.
Primary conversion: First DTC purchase, ideally on subscribe-and-save
Known constraints:
- Brand voice is non-negotiable. Irreverent metal/horror humor. No "wellness girl" energy. No corporate hydration claims.
- FDA constraints on health claims (it is water, not a supplement)
- Cannot show alcohol substitution in a way that implies medical sobriety claims
- Cans + plastic-kill messaging is a pillar — sustainability angle must be true, not greenwashed

## Notes

- Existing creator/celebrity content is the strongest performer historically. Studio creative needs to feel like it belongs in that universe.
- Subscription LTV is the primary unit economics lever. Acquisition cost can stretch if first-order → second-order conversion holds.
- Sparkling + Armless Palmer SKUs are newer than still water and have less brand recall, so they need their own creative lane.
- Sober-curious is a real segment but the brand never preaches it. The angle is "you can have a drink in your hand that doesn't suck" — implied, not lectured.
