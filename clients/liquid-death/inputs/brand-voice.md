# Brand Voice

## Voice

- Irreverent, dark-humor, metal-adjacent — the brand sounds like a horror comic that happens to sell water
- Confident, never apologetic, never "wellness"
- Treats the customer as in on the joke, never the target of it
- Pro-environment but never preachy — the plastic-kill message is delivered as villain origin story, not lecture
- Punchlines land in 3-5 words, not paragraphs

## Phrases To Use

- "Murder your thirst"
- "Death to plastic"
- "Healthy as hell"
- "Sell your soul" (used in brand campaigns)
- "Liquid Death" used as a verb when the joke supports it
- Skull, scythe, blood, tombstone imagery as a wink, not as horror

## Phrases To Avoid

- "Hydration journey" / "wellness" / "self-care"
- "Crisp, refreshing, premium"
- "Clean ingredients"
- Any sentence a Pepsi exec would write
- Earnest sustainability prose
- Any mention of "lifestyle" — show, don't say it
- Sober / sobriety language used clinically

## Tone Notes

The voice works because it is allowed to be funny first and a beverage brand second. AI-drafted copy fails when it tries to be "edgy but safe." If the line could appear on a Smartwater ad, it is wrong. The line should make a marketing director slightly uncomfortable and a 28-year-old at the gym snort.
