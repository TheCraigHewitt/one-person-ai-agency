# ICP Brief — Liquid Death

## Executive Summary

Liquid Death sells water as identity. The job-to-be-done is not hydration — it is "I want a drink in my hand that doesn't make me look like a wellness influencer or a guy who still drinks Bud Light." The primary acquisition target is 25-38, gym-coded, anti-plastic, irreverent, and tired of the binary between energy drinks and bottled water. The Meta paid social mandate is to recruit these buyers into a first DTC order and convert them to subscription within 30 days. Secondary segment is 21-30 festival/event-goers, who buy on aesthetic and convert worst on subscription but best on viral content.

## Primary Buyer Segments

**Segment A — Gym + sober-curious men, 28-38**
The economic engine. Drinks 2-4 cans/day, willing to subscribe, treats the can as an identity prop in the gym/office. Bought in because the brand feels like a permission slip to not drink alcohol without making it a personality.

**Segment B — Festival / event / "social drink replacement," 21-30**
Worse LTV, better virality. Buys multi-packs around specific events. Drives the UGC engine on TikTok and Instagram. Subscription conversion under 15%, but referral and content lift is the highest of any segment.

**Segment C — Anti-plastic / environmental signaler, 30-45**
Lower volume, highest brand loyalty. Buys for the household, treats Liquid Death as part of a kit alongside Hydro Flask, Patagonia, Allbirds. Reads the sustainability copy, actually finishes the donation page.

## Trigger Events

- Joined a new gym or training program in the last 60 days
- Quit or cut back on alcohol in the last 90 days
- Hosting or attending a multi-day event (festival, golf trip, bachelor party)
- Saw a creator post with the can in frame (highest single trigger)
- Recently switched to a cleaner diet or a tracker app (MyFitnessPal, MacroFactor, Whoop, Oura)

## Pain Points

- "Every other water brand markets like it's selling face cream"
- "I'm at the bar and I want a drink that doesn't make me explain why I'm not drinking"
- "I'm sick of the plastic bottle pile in my recycling bin"
- "Sparkling waters all taste like a memory of fruit"
- "Energy drinks feel like a step backwards from where I'm trying to go"

## Current Alternatives

- LaCroix / Waterloo / Spindrift (sparkling, mainstream, no identity)
- Smartwater (corporate, no story)
- Celsius (energy adjacent, gym-coded but caffeinated)
- Liquid I.V. + a Nalgene (functional, but ugly)
- Beer / White Claw (the thing they're trying to avoid)

## Buying Objections

- "$2/can is steep for water"
- "I can fill up a YETI for free"
- "The branding is too much for my office / family"
- "I tried it, the sparkling was fine but I'm not subscribing to water"
- "I get it for free from sampling at events"

## Message Angles

1. **The Bar Hand** — "A drink in your hand that doesn't make you explain yourself."
2. **The Gym Prop** — "What you actually drink between sets."
3. **The Plastic Body Count** — "Every can is one less bottle. Murder plastic, not the planet."
4. **The Subscription Lazy** — "Stop pretending you'll remember to reorder."
5. **The Anti-Wellness** — "Healthy as hell. No yoga mat required."

Angle 1 is the workhorse for new acquisition. Angle 2 is the gym-targeted retargeting angle. Angle 4 is the subscription nudge for second-purchase buyers.

## Meta Targeting Implications

- Lookalikes off DTC subscribers will outperform Lookalikes off all DTC buyers (subscription quality signal)
- Detailed targeting: gym chains, MMA, metal music, festivals, Liquid I.V., Athletic Greens — but lean on broad + creative as the real lever in 2026
- Advantage+ Shopping with creator UGC consistently beats studio creative
- Retargeting window: 14 days for first-purchase, 45 days for subscription pull

## Unknowns And Assumptions

- Assumes Q2 2026 first-purchase AOV holds at ~$40 — needs validation from client.
- Subscribe-and-save attach rate on first order is the lever we have least visibility into without ad account access. Confirm before scripting Hero Offer language.
- Retail cannibalization unknown — if a buyer already grabs Liquid Death at Whole Foods, the DTC offer has to do work to be the better choice. Likely a "convenience + customization" play, not a price play.
