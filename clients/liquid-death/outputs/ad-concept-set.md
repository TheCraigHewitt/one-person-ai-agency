# Ad Concept Set — Liquid Death

Five concepts. Each is paired with a recommended format (UGC vs studio), a target segment, and an honest risk note. The voice in the copy is Liquid Death's. The analysis around it is the agency's.

---

## Concept 1 — "The Bar Hand"

Concept name: The Bar Hand
Target segment: Segment A — gym + sober-curious men, 28-38
Core pain: Holding a drink at the bar that doesn't trigger questions
Hook: *"The drink in your hand answers questions before you do."*
Visual direction: 9:16 UGC. Bar setting, dim. Hand reaches into a cooler past three beers, pulls out a Liquid Death tall-boy. Crack. Pour. Single cut.
Primary text: "Order something they won't ask about."
Headline variants:
- "A drink that doesn't owe anyone an explanation."
- "Hold something. Say nothing."
- "Murder small talk. Murder your thirst."
CTA: "Get your first 8-pack"
Landing page alignment: Subscription-first hero. "Bar Hand" testimonial collection below fold.
Why it might work: Hits the strongest unspoken job-to-be-done in the gym + sober-curious segment. Identity reframe, no health claim. Sits inside brand voice without straying.
Risk / caveat: Bar-setting creative can read as alcohol-adjacent and trip Meta's automated review. Test a non-bar variant in parallel (kitchen, party, tailgate).

## Concept 2 — "Plastic Body Count"

Concept name: Plastic Body Count
Target segment: Segment C — anti-plastic / environmental, 30-45
Core pain: The visible recycling bin pile, the household guilt
Hook: *"Every can is one less body in the ocean."*
Visual direction: Studio. Pile of crushed plastic water bottles, slow zoom out. Single Liquid Death can placed on top. Slate: "1 can = 1 less bottle. Murder plastic, not the planet."
Primary text: "We're not a water company. We're a plastic-extinction event."
Headline variants:
- "Death to plastic. One can at a time."
- "Subscribe. Murder the bottle aisle."
- "The water that fights the water aisle."
CTA: "Start the subscription"
Landing page alignment: Sustainability story moves above the fold for traffic from this concept. Donation counter visible.
Why it might work: The plastic angle is one of the few honest sustainability stories in DTC beverage — aluminum is genuinely better. Lands strongest with the lower-volume, higher-LTV segment that household-buys.
Risk / caveat: Sustainability ads have lower CTR in cold audiences. Best run as a retargeting layer, not a cold acquisition test.

## Concept 3 — "Between Sets"

Concept name: Between Sets
Target segment: Segment A — gym + sober-curious men, 28-38
Core pain: Gym water bottle ugliness, energy drink fatigue
Hook: *"What you actually drink between sets."*
Visual direction: 9:16 UGC, gym. Creator shoots from squat rack, breathing hard. Reaches off-camera. Comes back with Liquid Death. Slugs it. Slams the can flat on a plate. Done.
Primary text: "Healthy as hell. Murder your thirst."
Headline variants:
- "Between sets. Between excuses."
- "The water that earns its place on the rack."
- "Sip something with a body count."
CTA: "Get your first 8-pack"
Landing page alignment: Hero variety pack, gym creator testimonials in the proof strip.
Why it might work: Strongest occasion-creative for the highest-LTV segment. Maps onto an existing organic content lane (gym + Liquid Death) so creator UGC is plentiful and cheap to produce.
Risk / caveat: Crowded category — Celsius is loud here. Differentiation has to come from voice, not occasion.

## Concept 4 — "Subscription Switch"

Concept name: Subscription Switch
Target segment: Segment A + B — anyone who bought once
Core pain: "I tried it, never reordered"
Hook: *"You bought one 8-pack. Now your fridge is sad."*
Visual direction: 9:16 studio. Top-down shot of a fridge with one stray Liquid Death rolling around. Door slams. Cut to subscription confirmation screen.
Primary text: "Stop pretending you'll remember to reorder."
Headline variants:
- "Murder the grocery run."
- "Set it. Forget it. Drink it."
- "Subscribe. Save 15%. Stop thinking about water."
CTA: "Set up your subscription"
Landing page alignment: Subscription-first hero. First-shipment date prominent. Cancel-anytime text large enough to read on mobile.
Why it might work: First-to-second-purchase is the lever the brand most needs to move. This concept owns that gap explicitly. Voice still on-brand.
Risk / caveat: Retargeting-only audience. Will look weak in cold-audience tests and may get killed prematurely by an algorithm that doesn't know its job.

## Concept 5 — "I Quit Beer"

Concept name: I Quit Beer
Target segment: Segment A — sober-curious men, 28-38
Core pain: "Everyone has an opinion about why I'm not drinking"
Hook: *"I quit beer. I didn't quit being fun."*
Visual direction: 9:16 UGC. Creator on a couch, beer commercial muted on the TV behind them. Pops a Liquid Death. Looks straight to camera. Beat. *"I quit beer. I didn't quit having a drink."*
Primary text: "Sober is the new metal."
Headline variants:
- "Quit drinking. Keep the can."
- "Murder beer. Save your liver."
- "The drink that earned its place at the bar."
CTA: "Get your first 8-pack"
Landing page alignment: Founder/brand-story variant of the page, leaning into the "we're not a wellness brand" line.
Why it might work: The sober-curious angle is real and growing. No competitor in the canned-water set is hitting it directly. Voice supports it without preaching.
Risk / caveat: Highest brand-safety risk in the set. Run through legal before testing. Avoid any implied sobriety-as-recovery claim — Liquid Death's lane is lifestyle, not therapy.

## Quality review — agency notes

Five concepts is the right number for a first sprint. Recommended test cadence:

- **Week 1:** Bar Hand + Between Sets (Segment A, highest-LTV bet)
- **Week 2:** Plastic Body Count (retargeting only) + Subscription Switch (retargeting only)
- **Week 3:** I Quit Beer, after legal review

Each concept should produce 3-5 hook variants for iteration. The hooks above are the lead variant. Foreplay-pulled creative patterns from Poppi and Spindrift should inform the variant set.

The brand voice was the gating constraint on all five concepts. Several alternates were cut because they read as "edgy but safe" — copy that would pass a Smartwater committee. Those don't work for this brand.
