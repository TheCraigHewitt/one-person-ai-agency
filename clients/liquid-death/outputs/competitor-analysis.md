## Positioning Patterns

- The category has split into two emotional poles: **calm** (Recess, Olipop, AG1) and **fuel** (Celsius, Liquid I.V., Liquid Death)
- Brands without a clear pole (Waterloo) are losing share of cultural conversation, not necessarily revenue
- Liquid Death is the only brand in either pole using horror/metal aesthetic — the white-space is real and durable

## Offer Patterns

- Multi-pack + subscribe-and-save is the universal default
- First-order discount is 15-20% across the set (Spindrift 15%, Poppi 20%, Olipop 15%, AG1 first-month $20 off + freebies)
- Liquid Death's first-order DTC offer is mild relative to the field — opportunity to test a sharper first-order hook
- No competitor is doing "merch with order" at scale — Liquid Death already has the brand to do this, underused in paid social

## Hook Patterns

The top-performing hook archetypes in the category right now (from Foreplay ad metadata + engagement signal):

1. **Identity reframe** — "I'm the kind of person who drinks X" (Liquid Death, Recess)
2. **Switch testimonial** — "I used to drink Y, now I drink X" (Poppi, Olipop, Liquid I.V.)
3. **Occasion stack** — "What I drink at the gym/work/festival" (Celsius, Liquid Death, AG1)
4. **Visual ingredient proof** — "Real fruit you can see in the can" (Spindrift) — Liquid Death cannot do this, water is water
5. **Anti-establishment punchline** — "Big Beverage hates this" (Liquid Death core)

## Creative Patterns

- UGC outperforms studio across every brand in the set, including Liquid Death
- Creator content with the product visible in hand in the first 1.5 seconds is the dominant winning format
- Static + carousel still gets distribution but losing share to 9:16 video
- "Founder talking to camera" works for Poppi and AG1, would not work for Liquid Death — the brand doesn't have a founder-as-face strategy and shouldn't start

## Landing Page Patterns

- Hero variety pack as the first SKU offer (everyone)
- "As seen at" retail logo strip near the fold (everyone except Recess)
- Sustainability story below the fold (Liquid Death, Olipop)
- Subscribe-and-save as the default toggle, not the optional toggle (AG1, Liquid I.V., Poppi)

## White Space

1. **No competitor is hitting the "I quit beer" angle directly.** Liquid Death dances around it. There is a clean Meta creative lane for "What I drink at the bar now."
2. **The merch-with-order pattern is unused** at scale. Liquid Death already has the brand to monetize this.
3. **Subscription as default, not toggle** — Liquid Death's checkout still asks the question. Most category leaders have stopped asking.
4. **Festival / event creator content** is underweight on Liquid Death's paid feed despite being the strongest organic territory.

## What To Steal

- AG1's testimonial length and structure (90-second talking-head with timestamps)
- Spindrift's "real ingredient close-up" cadence (translate to "real aluminum / real recycling" for Liquid Death)
- Poppi's switch-testimonial structure: "I used to drink ___, now I drink ___"

## What To Avoid

- Recess's pastel softness — same target, opposite voice, do not blend
- Olipop's health-claim-heavy copy — Liquid Death does not have, and should not invent, health claims
- Founder-talking-head — not the brand strategy, would feel off

## Unknowns And Assumptions

- Assumes Foreplay's "active ad" signal is current as of pull date (May 11, 2026)
- Engagement signal is a proxy for spend — Liquid I.V. and Poppi may be spending more than the ad count suggests
- Cannot see CPA or ROAS from competitor data — recommendations are creative-side only
